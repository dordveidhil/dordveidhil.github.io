Lake Superior Bathymetry

fter watching John Nelson's seafloor map tutorial, I decided to give recreating a similar effect in QGIS a try. I downloaded an incomplete geotiff of Lake Superior's bathymetry from NOAA and loaded the raster into QGIS. I then generated a hillshade layer using the Hillshade tool in the Terrain Analysis toolbox and reduced the transparency over the base bathymetry layer. Both layers used Q's base blues color ramp - this color ramp reminded me of the blues I've seen in Lake Superior's ice. I rotated the map in Q's layout manager and exported as a .png to load into GIMP. Once in GIMP I used a blur effect to soften the land surrounding the lake and a sharpen filter to enhance the 3D effect within the lake itself. I'm pretty pleased with the result. Date Accessed: 13 March 2025
